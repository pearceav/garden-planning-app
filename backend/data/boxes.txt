== In-Ground ==

Type                    | Best For              | Pros                                      | Cons
------------------------|-----------------------|-------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------------
Yard/Grass Plot         | Sprawling plants, large gardens | No cost, unlimited space, natural drainage | Sandy island soil may need amendments, weeds

== Raised Beds ==

Type                    | Best For              | Pros                                      | Cons
------------------------|-----------------------|-------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------------
Wood Raised Bed         | Most veggies, herbs   | Affordable, easy to build, good drainage  | Rots over time (cedar/redwood last longer)
Metal Raised Bed        | All plants            | Durable, modern look, no rot              | Can heat up in sun, more expensive
  Available sizes: 2ft x 2ft (circular), 4ft x 2ft, 6ft x 3ft ONLY — do not use other sizes
Stone/Brick Raised Bed  | Permanent gardens     | Very durable, retains heat                | Heavy, expensive, permanent

== Containers ==

Type                    | Best For                      | Notes
------------------------|-------------------------------|--------------------------------------------------
Terra Cotta Pots        | Herbs, flowers                | Classic look, breathable but dries out fast
Plastic Pots            | Most plants                   | Lightweight, retains moisture, affordable
Half Barrels            | Large plants, watermelon      | Deep root space, rustic look

== Depth Requirements ==

Deep containers (12+ inches):
- Tomatoes
- Peppers (bell, jalapeno)
- Cucumbers
- Carrots
- Okra

Wide/spreading room needed:
- Watermelon (very large)
- Cowpea
- String beans
- Snap peas

Shallow containers OK:
- Most herbs
- Marigolds
- Zinnias
- Linaria
